Our TOP 3 products

PACAMARA
Pacamara is a variety obtained from the cross between the Pacas and Maragogipe varieties originally made in El Salvador. Their offspring combines characteristics from the Pacas, such as low size, short internodes and good productivity, with large fruits and leaves from the Maragogipe variety. Pacamara presents variations in the size of the fruits and grain; although, the greatest production involves large, elongated fruits, similar to the fruits of the Maragogipe variety, which are red and the drink quality is excellent. Commercially, due to the size of the grain and the beverage quality, Pacamara is in high demand in specialty coffee markets and its quality is recognized with better prices.

ICATÚ
The word Icatú means 'bonanza' in the Tupi-Guarani language. This variety originated from a crossing made in Brazil in 1950 between Robusta and Bourbón from the red fruit, giving as a result the Arabusta variety. This variety was backcrossed several times with the 'Mundo Novo' variety to continue with the evaluation and selection of desirable and stable characteristics. In the year 1992, it made the official launch with the variety name Icatú for the Institute Agronomy of Campinas (IAC), Brazil, where it is reported currently as moderately susceptible to rust.

BOURBÓN
The plant of the Bourbón variety has a less pronounced conical shape, presenting more abundant secondary branches, shorter internodes and greater number of flowers. The shoots are green, the leaves are wider with wavy edges, the fruit is smaller, a little shorter, red when maturing, and has an excellent cup quality. Bourbón is very precocious in its maturation, with risk of falling fruit due to rain. Due to its development, it is typified as a variety of tall size (3 meters) being susceptible to strong winds but also features rust susceptibility. These characteristics motivated the Guatemalan producers to cultivate it.
